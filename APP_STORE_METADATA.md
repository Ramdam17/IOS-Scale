### Description (4000 characters max)
```
IOS Scale is a research-grade application for measuring intersubjective experiences using visual circle-based scales. Based on the scientifically validated Inclusion of Other in the Self (IOS) Scale, this app extends the original methodology with nine distinct measurement modalities.

MEASUREMENT MODALITIES

• Basic IOS — The classic two-circle overlap measurement, as described in the original research by Aron, Aron, and Smollan (1992)

• Advanced IOS — Two-circle overlap with independently adjustable circle sizes, capturing both closeness and perceived relative importance

• Overlap — Percentage-based overlap visualization for precise quantification

• Set Membership — Venn diagram style inclusion measurement

• Proximity — Distance-based measurement between entities

• Identification — Directional relationship from self to other

• Projection — Directional relationship from other to self

• Attribution — Attribute assignment between entities

• Observation — Third-party observer perspective measurement

FEATURES

• Normalized 0.0 to 1.0 scale across all modalities for consistent analysis
• Customizable labels for "Self" and "Other" entities
• Session history with detailed timestamps
• Data export in CSV, TSV, and JSON formats
• iCloud synchronization across all your devices
• Sign in with Apple for secure authentication
• Haptic feedback for intuitive interactions
• Dark mode support
• No ads, no tracking, no subscriptions

SCIENTIFIC FOUNDATION

This application is based on established research in intersubjective measurement:

• Aron, A., Aron, E. N., & Smollan, D. (1992). Inclusion of Other in the Self Scale and the Structure of Interpersonal Closeness. Journal of Personality and Social Psychology.

• Ollagnier-Beldame, M., & Coupé, C. (2019). Meeting You for the First Time: Descriptive Categories of an Intersubjective Experience. Constructivist Foundations.

• Coupé, C., & Ollagnier-Beldame, M. (2022). Diversity in Intersubjective Representations. Frontiers in Psychology.

IDEAL FOR

• Researchers in psychology and cognitive science
• Therapists and counselors
• Students studying interpersonal relationships
• Anyone interested in exploring subjective closeness

Your data stays private. All measurements are stored locally on your device and optionally synced via iCloud. We never access, sell, or share your data.
```
